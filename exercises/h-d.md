# Coarticulation Study: /h/ + /d/

### Sequence: voiceless glottal fricative → voiced alveolar stop

### Example Phrase: **he did**

**IPA:** `/hi dɪd/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[hi dɪd[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /h/ | glottal | fricative | voiceless | oral | brief, anticipates following vowel |
| Second | /d/ | alveolar | stop (plosive) | voiced | oral | variable, flapped intervocalically |

* **/h/**: Glottal → voiced alveolar
* **/d/**: Voiced alveolar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /h/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between glottal and alveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiceless to voiced. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /h/ terminates as the /d/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Glottal → voiced alveolar

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"he"** slowly, establishing full /h/ articulation.
2. Say **"did"** starting with an isolated /d/.
3. Merge: *he did* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/hi dɪd/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /h/ and /d/.
* Alternate "h-d-h-d" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
