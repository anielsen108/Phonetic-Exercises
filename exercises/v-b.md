## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the bilabial target before the /v/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between labiodental and bilabial articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /v/ and /b/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /v/ terminates as the /b/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Fricative ‚Üí stop, both voiced labial
