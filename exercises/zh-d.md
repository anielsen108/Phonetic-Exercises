## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the / í/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between postalveolar and alveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both / í/ and /d/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of / í/ terminates as the /d/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Voiced fricative ‚Üí voiced stop
