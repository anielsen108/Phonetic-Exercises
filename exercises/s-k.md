# Coarticulation Study: /s/ + /k/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar fricative → voiceless velar stop

### Example Phrase: **ask clearly**

**IPA:** `/æsk kʰliɹli/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[æsk kʰliɹli[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /s/ | alveolar | fricative (sibilant) | voiceless | oral | continuous, high-energy |
| Second | /k/ | velar | stop (plosive) | voiceless | oral | short, aspirated in stressed onsets |

* **/s/**: Alveolar → velar place change
* **/k/**: Voiceless velar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the /s/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and velar articulations.

2. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /s/ terminates as the /k/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Alveolar → velar place change

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"ask"** slowly, establishing full /s/ articulation.
2. Say **"clearly"** starting with an isolated /k/.
3. Merge: *ask clearly* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/æsk kʰliɹli/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /s/ and /k/.
* Alternate "s-k-s-k" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
