# Coarticulation Study: /t/ + /j/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar stop → palatal approximant

### Example Phrase: **at your place**

**IPA:** `/ætʃ jɚ pʰleɪs/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[ætʃ jɚ pʰleɪs[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /t/ | alveolar | stop (plosive) | voiceless | oral | short, often unreleased word-finally |
| Second | /j/ | palatal | approximant | voiced | oral | brief glide |

* **/t/**: Coalescence common: /t/+/j/ → [tʃ]
* **/j/**: Palatal approximant articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the palatal target before the /t/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and palatal articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiceless to voiced. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /t/ closure releases directly into the /j/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Coalescence common: /t/+/j/ → [tʃ]

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"at"** slowly, establishing full /t/ articulation.
2. Say **"your place"** starting with an isolated /j/.
3. Merge: *at your place* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/ætʃ jɚ pʰleɪs/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /t/ and /j/.
* Alternate "t-j-t-j" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
