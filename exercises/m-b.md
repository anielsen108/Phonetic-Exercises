# Coarticulation Study: /m/ + /b/

### Sequence: bilabial nasal → voiced bilabial stop

### Example Phrase: **some boys**

**IPA:** `/sʌm bɔɪz/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[sʌm bɔɪz[`

---

* **/m/**: Homorganic voiced bilabial
* **/b/**: Voiced bilabial stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /m/ and /b/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

2. **Nasal Coarticulation:**
   Velum movement coordinates with oral articulation. Nasal airflow may extend into or anticipate the adjacent segment.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Homorganic voiced bilabial

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"some"** slowly, establishing full /m/ articulation.
2. Say **"boys"** starting with an isolated /b/.
3. Merge: *some boys* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/sʌm bɔɪz/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /m/ and /b/.
* Alternate "m-b-m-b" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
