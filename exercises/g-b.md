# Coarticulation Study: /g/ + /b/

### Sequence: voiced velar stop → voiced bilabial stop

### Example Phrase: **big boy**

**IPA:** `/bɪɡ̚ bɔɪ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[bɪɡ̚ bɔɪ[`

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the bilabial target before the /g/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between velar and bilabial articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /g/ and /b/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Stop Release Overlap:**
   The /g/ is typically unreleased ([g̚]), with air pressure maintained until the /b/ closure forms. The release burst belongs to /b/.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Voiced sequence maintained

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"big"** slowly, establishing full /g/ articulation.
2. Say **"boy"** starting with an isolated /b/.
3. Merge: *big boy* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/bɪɡ̚ bɔɪ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /g/ and /b/.
* Alternate "g-b-g-b" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
