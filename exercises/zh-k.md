1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the / í/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between postalveolar and velar articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiced to voiceless. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of / í/ terminates as the /k/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.
