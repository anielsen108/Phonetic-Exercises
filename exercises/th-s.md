# Coarticulation Study: /θ/ + /s/

### Sequence: voiceless dental fricative → voiceless alveolar fricative

### Example Phrase: **baths**

**IPA:** `/bæθs/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[bæθs[`

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |

* **/θ/**: Dental → alveolar fricative sequence
* **/s/**: Voiceless alveolar fricative articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /θ/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between dental and alveolar articulations.

2. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Dental → alveolar fricative sequence

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"baths"** slowly, establishing full /θ/ articulation.
2. Say **"s"** starting with an isolated /s/.
3. Merge: *baths* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/bæθs/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /θ/ and /s/.
* Alternate "θ-s-θ-s" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
