# Coarticulation Study: /k/ + /θ/

### Sequence: voiceless velar stop → voiceless dental fricative

### Example Phrase: **back three**

**IPA:** `/bæk̪̚ θ̪ɹi/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[bæk̪̚ θ̪ɹi[`

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the dental target before the /k/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between velar and dental articulations.

2. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /k/ closure releases directly into the /θ/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Velar → dental place shift

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"back"** slowly, establishing full /k/ articulation.
2. Say **"three"** starting with an isolated /θ/.
3. Merge: *back three* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/bæk̪̚ θ̪ɹi/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /k/ and /θ/.
* Alternate "k-θ-k-θ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
