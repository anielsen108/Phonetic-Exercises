# Coarticulation Study: /d/ + /tʃ/

### Sequence: voiced alveolar stop → voiceless postalveolar affricate

### Example Phrase: **bad choice**

**IPA:** `/bæd̥̚ tʃɔɪs/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[bæd̥̚ tʃɔɪs[`

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the postalveolar target before the /d/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and postalveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiced to voiceless. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /d/ closure releases directly into the /tʃ/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Devoicing before voiceless affricate

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"bad"** slowly, establishing full /d/ articulation.
2. Say **"choice"** starting with an isolated /tʃ/.
3. Merge: *bad choice* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/bæd̥̚ tʃɔɪs/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /d/ and /tʃ/.
* Alternate "d-tʃ-d-tʃ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
