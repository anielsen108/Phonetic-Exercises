# Coarticulation Study: /l/ + /θ/

### Sequence: alveolar lateral approximant → voiceless dental fricative

### Example Phrase: **health**

**IPA:** `/hɛɫθ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[hɛɫθ[`

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the dental target before the /l/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and dental articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiced to voiceless. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Lateral before dental fricative

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"health"** slowly, establishing full /l/ articulation.
2. Say **"θ"** starting with an isolated /θ/.
3. Merge: *health* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/hɛɫθ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /l/ and /θ/.
* Alternate "l-θ-l-θ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
