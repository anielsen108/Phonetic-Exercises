## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the bilabial target before the /l/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and bilabial articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /l/ and /b/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Dark lateral ‚Üí bilabial

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"will"** slowly, establishing full /l/ articulation.
2. Say **"be"** starting with an isolated /b/.
3. Merge: *will be* ‚Äî maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /l/ and /b/.
* Alternate "l-b-l-b" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
