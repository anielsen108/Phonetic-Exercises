1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the palatal target before the /d/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and palatal articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /d/ and /j/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /d/ closure releases directly into the /j/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.
