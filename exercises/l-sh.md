# Coarticulation Study: /l/ + /ʃ/

### Sequence: alveolar lateral approximant → voiceless postalveolar fricative

### Example Phrase: **will show**

**IPA:** `/wɪɫ ʃoʊ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[wɪɫ ʃoʊ[`

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the postalveolar target before the /l/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and postalveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiced to voiceless. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Lateral → postalveolar

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"will"** slowly, establishing full /l/ articulation.
2. Say **"show"** starting with an isolated /ʃ/.
3. Merge: *will show* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/wɪɫ ʃoʊ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /l/ and /ʃ/.
* Alternate "l-ʃ-l-ʃ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
