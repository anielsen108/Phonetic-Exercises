# Coarticulation Study: /st/ + /z/

### Sequence: fricative + stop cluster → voiced alveolar fricative

### Example Phrase: **just zone**

**IPA:** `/dʒʌst̚ zoʊn/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[dʒʌst̚ zoʊn[`

* **/st/**: Voiceless → voiced sibilant

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiceless to voiced. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

2. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /st/ closure releases directly into the /z/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Voiceless → voiced sibilant

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"just"** slowly, establishing full /st/ articulation.
2. Say **"zone"** starting with an isolated /z/.
3. Merge: *just zone* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/dʒʌst̚ zoʊn/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /st/ and /z/.
* Alternate "st-z-st-z" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
