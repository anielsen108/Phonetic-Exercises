## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /g/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between velar and alveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /g/ and /d/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Stop Release Overlap:**
   The /g/ is typically unreleased ([gÃö]), with air pressure maintained until the /d/ closure forms. The release burst belongs to /d/.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
