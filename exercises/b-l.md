## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /b/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between bilabial and alveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /b/ and /l/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /b/ closure releases directly into the /l/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Lateral release, syllabic
