# Coarticulation Study: /l/ + /g/

### Sequence: alveolar lateral approximant → voiced velar stop

### Example Phrase: **will go**

**IPA:** `/wɪɫ ɡoʊ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[wɪɫ ɡoʊ[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /l/ | alveolar | lateral approximant | voiced | oral (lateral) | continuous, velarized finally |
| Second | /g/ | velar | stop (plosive) | voiced | oral | short, often reduced finally |

* **/l/**: Velarized lateral → velar stop
* **/g/**: Voiced velar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the /l/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and velar articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /l/ and /g/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Velarized lateral → velar stop

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"will"** slowly, establishing full /l/ articulation.
2. Say **"go"** starting with an isolated /g/.
3. Merge: *will go* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/wɪɫ ɡoʊ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /l/ and /g/.
* Alternate "l-g-l-g" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
