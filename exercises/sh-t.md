# Coarticulation Study: /ʃ/ + /t/

### Sequence: voiceless postalveolar fricative → voiceless alveolar stop

### Example Phrase: **wash time**

**IPA:** `/wɑʃ tʰaɪm/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[wɑʃ tʰaɪm[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /ʃ/ | postalveolar | fricative (sibilant) | voiceless | oral | continuous, lower pitch than /s/ |
| Second | /t/ | alveolar | stop (plosive) | voiceless | oral | short, often unreleased word-finally |

* **/ʃ/**: Postalveolar → alveolar: tongue moves forward
* **/t/**: Voiceless alveolar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /ʃ/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between postalveolar and alveolar articulations.

2. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /ʃ/ terminates as the /t/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Postalveolar → alveolar: tongue moves forward

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"wash"** slowly, establishing full /ʃ/ articulation.
2. Say **"time"** starting with an isolated /t/.
3. Merge: *wash time* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/wɑʃ tʰaɪm/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /ʃ/ and /t/.
* Alternate "ʃ-t-ʃ-t" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
