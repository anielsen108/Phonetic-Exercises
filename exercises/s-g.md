# Coarticulation Study: /s/ + /g/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar fricative → voiced velar stop

### Example Phrase: **this guy**

**IPA:** `/ðɪz ɡaɪ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[ðɪz ɡaɪ[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /s/ | alveolar | fricative (sibilant) | voiceless | oral | continuous, high-energy |
| Second | /g/ | velar | stop (plosive) | voiced | oral | short, often reduced finally |

* **/s/**: Voiced fricative → voiced stop
* **/g/**: Voiced velar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the /s/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and velar articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiceless to voiced. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /s/ terminates as the /g/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Voiced fricative → voiced stop

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"this"** slowly, establishing full /s/ articulation.
2. Say **"guy"** starting with an isolated /g/.
3. Merge: *this guy* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/ðɪz ɡaɪ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /s/ and /g/.
* Alternate "s-g-s-g" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
