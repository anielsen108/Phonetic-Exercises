## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the bilabial target before the /st/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and bilabial articulations.

2. **Stop Release Overlap:**
   The /st/ is typically unreleased ([stÃö]), with air pressure maintained until the /p/ closure forms. The release burst belongs to /p/.

3. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /st/ terminates as the /p/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.
