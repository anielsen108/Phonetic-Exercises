# Coarticulation Study: /θ/ + /k/

### Sequence: voiceless dental fricative → voiceless velar stop

### Example Phrase: **bath cloth**

**IPA:** `/bæθ̚ kʰlɔθ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[bæθ̚ kʰlɔθ[`

* **/θ/**: Dental → velar place shift
* **/k/**: Voiceless velar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the /θ/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between dental and velar articulations.

2. **Fricative-to-Stop Transition:**
   The continuous airflow of /θ/ terminates as the /k/ closure forms. Listeners perceive a sharp acoustic boundary.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Dental → velar place shift

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"bath"** slowly, establishing full /θ/ articulation.
2. Say **"cloth"** starting with an isolated /k/.
3. Merge: *bath cloth* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/bæθ̚ kʰlɔθ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /θ/ and /k/.
* Alternate "θ-k-θ-k" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
