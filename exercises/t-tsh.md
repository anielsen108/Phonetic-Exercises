# Coarticulation Study: /t/ + /tʃ/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar stop → voiceless postalveolar affricate

### Example Phrase: **at church**

**IPA:** `/æt̚ tʃɝtʃ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[æt̚ tʃɝtʃ[`

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the postalveolar target before the /t/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and postalveolar articulations.

2. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /t/ closure releases directly into the /tʃ/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Alveolar stop to post-alveolar affricate: place shift during release

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"at"** slowly, establishing full /t/ articulation.
2. Say **"church"** starting with an isolated /tʃ/.
3. Merge: *at church* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/æt̚ tʃɝtʃ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /t/ and /tʃ/.
* Alternate "t-tʃ-t-tʃ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
