## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the labiodental target before the /tʃ/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between postalveolar and labiodental articulations.

2. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Affricate → labiodental

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"much"** slowly, establishing full /tʃ/ articulation.
2. Say **"fun"** starting with an isolated /f/.
3. Merge: *much fun* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/mʌtʃ fʌn/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /tʃ/ and /f/.
* Alternate "tʃ-f-tʃ-f" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
