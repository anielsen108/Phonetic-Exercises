# Coarticulation Study: /tʃ/ + /k/

### Sequence: voiceless postalveolar affricate → voiceless velar stop

### Example Phrase: **which car**

**IPA:** `/wɪtʃ kʰɑɹ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[wɪtʃ kʰɑɹ[`

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the /tʃ/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between postalveolar and velar articulations.

2. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Affricate → velar stop

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"which"** slowly, establishing full /tʃ/ articulation.
2. Say **"car"** starting with an isolated /k/.
3. Merge: *which car* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/wɪtʃ kʰɑɹ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /tʃ/ and /k/.
* Alternate "tʃ-k-tʃ-k" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
