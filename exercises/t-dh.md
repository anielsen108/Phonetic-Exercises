# Coarticulation Study: /t/ + /ð/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar stop → voiced dental fricative

### Example Phrase: **at the store**

**IPA:** `/æt̪̚ ð̪ə ˈstoɹ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[æt̪̚ ð̪ə ˈstoɹ[`

---

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /t/ | alveolar | stop (plosive) | voiceless | oral | short, often unreleased word-finally |
| Second | /ð/ | dental | fricative | voiced | oral | continuous, common in function words |

* **/t/**: Dentalized unreleased /t̪̚/: tongue tip contact moves forward to teeth anticipating /ð̪/
* **/ð/**: Voiced dental fricative articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the dental target before the /t/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and dental articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiceless to voiced. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /t/ closure releases directly into the /ð/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Dentalized unreleased /t̪̚/: tongue tip contact moves forward to teeth anticipating /ð̪/

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"at"** slowly, establishing full /t/ articulation.
2. Say **"the store"** starting with an isolated /ð/.
3. Merge: *at the store* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/æt̪̚ ð̪ə ˈstoɹ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /t/ and /ð/.
* Alternate "t-ð-t-ð" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
