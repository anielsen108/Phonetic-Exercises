## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /r/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar/postalveolar and alveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /r/ and /d/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Rhotic + voiced stop

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"heard"** slowly, establishing full /r/ articulation.
2. Say **"d"** starting with an isolated /d/.
3. Merge: *heard* ‚Äî maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/h…ùd/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /r/ and /d/.
* Alternate "r-d-r-d" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
