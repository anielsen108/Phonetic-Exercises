## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the velar target before the /b/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between bilabial and velar articulations.

2. **Voicing Continuity:**
   Both /b/ and /g/ are voiced; the glottis remains adducted throughout. Spectrographically, this appears as a continuous low-frequency voicing bar.

3. **Stop Release Overlap:**
   The /b/ is typically unreleased ([bÃö]), with air pressure maintained until the /g/ closure forms. The release burst belongs to /g/.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
