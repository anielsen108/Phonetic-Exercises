# Coarticulation Study: /s/ + /tʃ/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar fricative → voiceless postalveolar affricate

### Example Phrase: **this choice**

**IPA:** `/ðɪʃ tʃɔɪs/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[ðɪʃ tʃɔɪs[`

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the postalveolar target before the /s/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and postalveolar articulations.

2. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Coalescence to [ʃ]

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"this"** slowly, establishing full /s/ articulation.
2. Say **"choice"** starting with an isolated /tʃ/.
3. Merge: *this choice* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/ðɪʃ tʃɔɪs/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /s/ and /tʃ/.
* Alternate "s-tʃ-s-tʃ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
