# Coarticulation Study: /s/ + /θ/

### Sequence: voiceless alveolar fricative → voiceless dental fricative

### Example Phrase: **this thing**

**IPA:** `/ðɪs̪ θ̪ɪŋ/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[ðɪs̪ θ̪ɪŋ[`

---

| Second | /θ/ | dental | fricative | voiceless | oral | continuous |

* **/s/**: Slight dentalization of /s̪/
* **/θ/**: Voiceless dental fricative articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the dental target before the /s/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and dental articulations.

2. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Slight dentalization of /s̪/

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"this"** slowly, establishing full /s/ articulation.
2. Say **"thing"** starting with an isolated /θ/.
3. Merge: *this thing* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/ðɪs̪ θ̪ɪŋ/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /s/ and /θ/.
* Alternate "s-θ-s-θ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
