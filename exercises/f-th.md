# Coarticulation Study: /f/ + /θ/

### Sequence: voiceless labiodental fricative → voiceless dental fricative

### Example Phrase: **if things**

**IPA:** `/ɪf̪ θ̪ɪŋz/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[ɪf̪ θ̪ɪŋz[`

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the dental target before the /f/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between labiodental and dental articulations.

2. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Labiodental → dental

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"if"** slowly, establishing full /f/ articulation.
2. Say **"things"** starting with an isolated /θ/.
3. Merge: *if things* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/ɪf̪ θ̪ɪŋz/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /f/ and /θ/.
* Alternate "f-θ-f-θ" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
