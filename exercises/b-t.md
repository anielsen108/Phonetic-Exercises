# Coarticulation Study: /b/ + /t/

### Sequence: voiced bilabial stop → voiceless alveolar stop

### Example Phrase: **cab trip**

**IPA:** `/kʰæb̥̚ tʰɹɪp/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[kʰæb̥̚ tʰɹɪp[`

---

## 1. Segmental Foundations

| Segment | Symbol | Place | Manner | Voicing | Airflow | Duration |
|----------|---------|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| First | /b/ | bilabial | stop (plosive) | voiced | oral | short closure, often unreleased |
| Second | /t/ | alveolar | stop (plosive) | voiceless | oral | short, often unreleased word-finally |

* **/b/**: Devoicing + place shift
* **/t/**: Voiceless alveolar stop articulation follows with typical place and manner characteristics.

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the alveolar target before the /b/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between bilabial and alveolar articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiced to voiceless. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Stop Release Overlap:**
   The /b/ is typically unreleased ([b̚]), with air pressure maintained until the /t/ closure forms. The release burst belongs to /t/.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Devoicing + place shift

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"cab"** slowly, establishing full /b/ articulation.
2. Say **"trip"** starting with an isolated /t/.
3. Merge: *cab trip* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/kʰæb̥̚ tʰɹɪp/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /b/ and /t/.
* Alternate "b-t-b-t" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
