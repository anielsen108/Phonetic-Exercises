# Coarticulation Study: /st/ + /f/

### Sequence: fricative + stop cluster → voiceless labiodental fricative

### Example Phrase: **first five**

**IPA:** `/fɝst̚ faɪv/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[fɝst̚ faɪv[`

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the labiodental target before the /st/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between alveolar and labiodental articulations.

2. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /st/ closure releases directly into the /f/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

3. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Cluster → labiodental

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"first"** slowly, establishing full /st/ articulation.
2. Say **"five"** starting with an isolated /f/.
3. Merge: *first five* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/fɝst̚ faɪv/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /st/ and /f/.
* Alternate "st-f-st-f" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
