# Coarticulation Study: /k/ + /j/

### Sequence: voiceless velar stop → palatal approximant

### Example Phrase: **back yard**

**IPA:** `/bæk jɑɹd/`
**Allophonic detail:** `[bæk jɑɹd[`

---

## 2. Coarticulatory Mechanisms

1. **Anticipatory Place Shift:**
   The tongue/lips begin moving toward the palatal target before the /k/ gesture completes. This creates overlap between velar and palatal articulations.

2. **Voicing Transition:**
   The glottis shifts from voiceless to voiced. This transition may show partial devoicing or voicing anticipation depending on speech rate.

3. **Stop Release Modification:**
   The /k/ closure releases directly into the /j/ articulation, often showing reduced or absent burst energy.

4. **Specific Adaptation:**
   Velar → palatal approximant

## 4. Articulatory Practice

### 4.1 Slow-Motion Drill

1. Say **"back"** slowly, establishing full /k/ articulation.
2. Say **"yard"** starting with an isolated /j/.
3. Merge: *back yard* — maintain voicing continuity, minimize release between segments.
4. Record and compare:

   * Over-released: Excessive separation between sounds
   * Balanced: Natural liaison as transcribed `/bæk jɑɹd/`
   * Under-articulated: Loss of primary articulatory cues

### 4.2 Gestural Continuity Exercise

* Focus awareness on active articulators for both /k/ and /j/.
* Alternate "k-j-k-j" while maintaining voicing where applicable.
* Notice the articulatory transition: where does one gesture end and the next begin?
* Perform slowly, then gradually increase speed while preserving clarity.
* The movement should feel **fluid** and **economical**, not **segmented**.
